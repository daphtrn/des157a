Final Project Report

Introduction

Summary of Key Findings

Overall, users found the usability of the project pretty straightforward. There are a few minor things that can be improved on. These include:

  • Exiting overlay by pressing outside the overlay.
  • Unsure if they should click or the computer first or pictures first.
  • Photos blend into the background a little.
  • More interactivity.

Users tested

  • Dad, 66 years old
  • Close friend, 23 years old

I wanted my target audience to be people that are close to me that know Justin to some extent. My intention with this project is to give people a better idea of who Justin is and my relationship with him. If they’re someone who knows him really well, like my family, then I want this project to serve as a way to appreciate my brother.

Task list

  • Click through photos

Results

Usability problems

  • Exiting overlay by pressing outside the overlay:

It is intuitive for the users to automatically press outside of the overlay to exit, rather than navigating to a “close” button every time. This is based on previous encounters with this in other applications.

  • Exiting overlay by pressing outside the overlay:

May be important to make it obvious to the user that it is helpful to turn to the computer first for context before going through the pictures.

Other feedback

  • Exiting overlay by pressing outside the overlay:

Make the white border on the photos a little darker so it stands out against the background.

  • Exiting overlay by pressing outside the overlay:

The current project is pretty simple, and it would benefit from more interactivity.
